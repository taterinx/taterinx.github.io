Actual Event Stuff

Are children allowed?
Due to the nature of the events, no.
Do you have a registry/accept gifts?
We have no registry and there is absolutely no obligation to gift anything! If you’d like, however, we do accept contributions to our VISA fund (going live 21st February) to help us live together sooner, and we’re happy to receive gifts at either of the events.
Is there any parking?

If you're attending the Chicago event, you likely already have an idea. There's no guarantee there will be an empty space along the block but you'll absolutely find somewhere. If you don't mind dressing fancy on the CTA, though, then we'd recommend that (the house is 5 minutes from Western/Damen).

We're still discussing venues for the UK event, so at the moment we can't give any advice on parking.

What is the dress code?

Technically, they call it cocktail attire/semi-formal. Sleek suits, dress shoes, heels or a cute party dress. It's "formal" without all the gross Linkedin Twat stuffiness.

Just be aware that if you're coming to the Chicago event and want to go into the backyard, heels aren’t compatible with the gravel and there may be dog poop. There may also be dogs. We haven’t established if the dogs are invited yet.

That aside, dress tuff.

Why is the ceremony itself private?
There are a few different reasons. The short answer: Privacy. It also gave us a lot of planning power.
Why are you hosting multiple events?

Maintaining the transatlantic peace.
